1. SITUATION: In the past 120 hours Narian Forces have attacked west, seizing the town of Darby as they continue their advance to Columbus. The 2nd Narian Infantry BDE currently occupies Darby with one BN forward in the disruption zone, one battalion to the East securing LOCs, and one BN dedicated to its defense. Additionally, the DIV mechanized reconnaissance company is screening in the disruption zone. UAS footage also shows a Narian Mechanized Infantry Company (BMP equipped) operating in the AO with two platoons vic Darby and one platoon in the disruption zone. Because of enemy knowledge of US Forces at Columbus APOD provided by civilian looking recon teams and HUMINT from the locals, the Narian BN in the disruption zone has identified the SBCT as the biggest threat to their BDE’s refit operations. Subsequently, they have chosen to defend IVO the improved/hard surface roads that approach Darby from the west. Three platoons in non-mutual supporting BPs allows them to defend both air and ground approach routes along I-185 to Buena Vista Rd to Darby as well as the I-185 to Hwy 27 to Hwy 26 to Hwy 137 route to Darby and this second route’s potential bypass route IVO Bush Hill. The 101st Airborne Division has been deployed to the Fort Benning/Columbus AO via Columbus Airport to prevent Narian Forces from continuing their attack to the northwest. 3rd BDE’s task is to destroy the enemy in their disruption zone, pass 1st BDE, 25th ID (who is currently in Columbus APOD conducting RSOI) through zone to facilitate the destruction of Narian Forces at Darby they will then pass 3rd BDE, 3rd ID through zone to facilitate the destruction of the Narian Forces along the international boundary. 3rd BDE, 101st ABN is currently operating vic Columbus APOD. 3/101st and 1/25th will conduct an attack to destroy forces IVO Darby and pass 3/3rd to destroy forces along the IB before the enemy can resume offensive operations.

1A. ENEMY FORCES: See Appendix 1, Annex B

COMPOSITION: The enemy company opposing us is expected to be near 85 percent strength. The company consists of three Infantry platoons, its organic weapons platoon, an attached weapons platoon from the heavy weapons company, an attached MIP (BMP-2 equipped) and a 2S6M. Each platoon has technical vehicles with (3) additional PKMs (mounted), approximately (38) personnel, (6) PZF3T600, (1) SA-18, (2) RPG-29, (6) dismounted PKMs and their basic small arms. In addition, it is expected that the company commander has attached a squad from the attached weapons platoon to each infantry platoon. These squads consist of (10) personnel with their organic weapons and a technical vehicle with a mounted W-87. The company’s organic weapons platoon is equipped with (3) 82mm mortars, (1) SA-18, and can be expected to possess vehicles to aid in mobility.

DISPOSITION: The purpose of the Narian forces that have seized Darby is to allow the build up of combat power of the Narian Brigade. Decisive to this enemy operation is to retain Darby proper for 96 hours. This is decisive because they believe it will stall U.S. forces long enough to accumulate combat power necessary to resume offensive operations toward Columbus. The enemy will accomplish this by establishing an area defense around Darby augmented by a disruption zone to the west. The enemy brigade we face is arrayed with one infantry battalion (decisive operation) retaining Darby in order to facilitate the buildup of combat power. One infantry battalion (shaping operation 1) establishes a disruption zone west of Darby and disrupts in order to prevent massing against the brigade decisive operation. One infantry battalion (shaping operation 2) establishes a support zone and secures key logistical routes East of Darby in order to facilitate passage of combat power across the international border and prevent the envelopment of the BDE DO from the north. Finally, one mechanized infantry company (minus) (shaping operation 3) blocks in vicinity of Darby in order to prevent the envelopment of the brigade decisive operation from the south.

The enemy battalion we face is arrayed with one infantry company (decisive operation) disrupting north of Darby vic HWY 22/80 and HWY 315 in order to prevent massing against the brigade decisive operation. One mechanized reconnaissance company (shaping operation 1) conducts a screen vicinity of the impact area to disrupt US Forces in order to prevent the bypass of the BN DO. One infantry company (shaping operation 2) disrupts along approach routes in order to prevent the bypass of the BN DO. Finally, one infantry company (shaping operation 3) blocks south of Darby (vic HWY 26) in order to prevent the envelopment of the BN DO.

The company we oppose is in the disruption zone, focused on the Red Diamond Rd, Buena Vista Rd, and the Hwy 137 avenues of approach. The enemy platoon reinforced battle positions allow him freedom of maneuver in positioning elements forward including OPs and limited counter reconnaissance mounted security patrols seeking to conduct asymmetrical disruption operations vic Columbus APOD within the next 96 hours. These enemy elements may be team sized and may use civilian looking pickup trucks along enemy over-watched routes to infiltrate west towards Columbus.

The Company directly opposing us has been in position for 72-96 hours and is located at the following positions:

• Platoons located vicinity GL161828, GL185845, and GL169778

• OP with possible SA-18 templated at GL157836, GL 172862, and GL 149787

• Possible Mortar Firing Points vic GL 179800 and GL 188830.

Other suspected unit locations within the battalion are indicated on the SITTEMP (Tab D to Appendix 1 to Annex B, SITTEMP).

CAPABILITIES:

a. Intelligence. The company opposing us will employ OP’s and limited patrols in the areas surrounding their BPs. It is unlikely we will see any additional assets from outside the company in sector to assist the company with reconnaissance, but the commander may employ OPs and FOs well forward of his positions on key terrain.

b. Movement & Maneuver. Reinforcements. The Co CDR will likely use the MIP (3 x BMP, approximately 30 personnel) as a company reserve. This platoon is capable of reinforcing any of the three platoons within 25-30 minutes of notification however it is unlikely that the commander will choose to reinforce one platoon with another because this will leave an avenue of approach undefended. The MIP was last spotted vic GL 210813. See SITTEMP for possible reinforcement routes. Additionally, intel recently has indicated the emplacement of an MT-12 anti-tank gun on the most dominant terrain in the enemy’s disruption zone, OBJ BULL.

c. Fire Support. The company we are facing has 82mm mortars in support. Expect the enemy to use illumination periodically during the night to reduce our night vision advantage. His use of illum will increase significantly upon identification of any US forces night operations. The BN CDR can launch up to (6) HIND-Ds sorties (30 minutes to the southeast) and can gain local air superiority for up to 30 minutes, however we do not expect to see rotary wing (CCA) until significant vehicular forces begin to move into sector.

d. Force Protection. For air defense, the company in our zone has (3-5) SA-18s man-packed ADA systems possibly forward or in their battle positions, as well as an attached 2S6M from the brigade. The 2S6M is templated with the Company CP at Cactus Landing Strip, allowing him flexibility to occupy prepared positions at each of the platoon locations. These assets will be positioned where he feels he can best interdict our air assets to prevent the build up of combat power through the air. The Narian forces have limited engineering assets although they have been in location for at least 72-96 hours, so we expect well developed battle positions within infantry platoon reinforced capabilities. Expect the enemy to have any static ADA systems and technical vehicles well concealed to protect them from US artillery. The infantry will have fighting positions with limited overhead cover and potentially shallow trench systems completed on the objectives. Protective obstacles on the objectives will consist of sparse AP mines, tangle foot, and a barbed wire or chain-link type fence. The enemy also has limited capability to emplace AT mines at key road intersections, and he may choose to employ IEDs along the routes as well. Expect to see the use of IEDs heaviest around the APOD and objective areas on remote roads.

e. Sustainment. The company we face will have enough supplies to last for an additional 96 hours. The enemy will be unable to conduct MEDEVAC during the battle, and will not likely be re-supplied once in contact. f. Command and Control. Signal intercepts from the enemy company commander were last traced to a six-digit grid vicinity of Cactus Landing Strip. The range and the terrain will adversely affect enemy FM communications between the platoons and the reserve element. The enemy employs a CINCGARS type FM radio with single channel cipher text capability. Additionally, the enemy is equipped with commercial cell phones and Motorola SP-21 UHF/VHF radios. This will probably result in effective overall command and control by the commander, and not affect reaction time for the commitment of reserve forces. The platoons will most likely have wire, FM and cell phone communications and should be able to establish effective C2 internally.

Most Probable Course of Action (see Appendix 3 to Annex B (SITTEMP). The purpose of the enemy operations in the disruption zone is to prevent the massing of combat power against the BN DO. Decisive to the enemy company reinforced we face is the destruction of 3 U.S. military vehicles between BM 155 and 184 along Buena Vista Road. This is decisive because the enemy believes it will force the U.S. to use multiple avenues of approach and piece-meal their attack through the disruption zone towards the town of Darby. The enemy will accomplish this by defending in sector from non-mutually supporting platoon battle positions along air and ground avenues of approach. The enemy will accept risk in fighting from non-mutually supporting positions and mitigate this risk by maintaining a reserve capable of reinforcing the platoon of choice. One platoon (decisive operation) will disrupt US forces along the Buena Vista Road in order to prevent bypass of the BN decisive operation; one platoon (shaping effort 1) will disrupt US forces using the avenues of approach vicinity Bush Hill in order to prevent the envelopment of the company DO from the south; and one platoon (shaping operation 2) will block US forces along Hwy 137 in order to prevent US forces from bypassing the company decisive operation. The purpose of mortars is to disrupt mounted forces, then to suppress dismounted infantry/engineers to break up friendly forces formations. The purpose of aviation is to destroy massed vehicular formations to prevent their organized attack at the town of Darby. The purpose of air defense is to destroy aircraft in order to prevent the buildup of combat power and resupply at the Columbus APOD. The enemy will employ OPs forward to provide early warning, to attempt to disrupt U.S. operations with mortar fires, and to gather intel on U.S. composition, disposition, strength, and activities. The enemy platoons will employ point, subsurface and surface laid minefields consisting of a mix of AP/AT mines and wire. The Company Commander will position his reserve to the east and will give them a priority for planning to reinforce the northern platoon, the center platoon and then the southern platoon. He will likely commit them to the platoon who faces the most significant US force or to the platoon whose protective obstacles are breached first. Critical to the enemy’s operation is the ability to identify the direction of the U.S. decisive operation. The enemy believes at endstate, U.S. forces will be unable to mass a company size force or greater through the disruption zone towards Darby and that Narian forces will retain control of all major avenues of approach in the AO.

3.B. CONCEPT OF THE OPERATION: (See Appendix 3 to Annex C)

The purpose of this operation is to pass the 1st BDE, 25th ID through zone and facilitate their seizure of the town of Darby. We will accomplish this by conducting a penetration. Decisive to this operation is the seizure of Bush Hill on OBJECTIVE BULL; this is decisive because this terrain controls multiple ground avenues of approach through our zone, thus allowing the passage of the 1st BDE, 25th ID onto the town of Darby. A Company(+) (shaping operation) fixes the enemy on OBJECTIVE ANT (GL185845) to prevent the enemy from repositioning against the battalion’s decisive operation. B Company(+) (BN decisive operation) seizes OBJECTIVE BULL (GL163828) in order to pass the 1st BDE, 25th ID through zone and facilitate their seizure of the town of Darby. C Company (-) (shaping operation ) fixes enemy forces vicinity OBJECTIVE COW (GL169778) in order to prevent enemy reinforcement against the BN decisive operation from the south. D Company (-) screens along the LD to prevent enemy forces from disrupting preparation for combat and initial movement. 1/C/1-187 IN follows the decisive operation as the battalion reserve with priorities for planning first to reinforce the BN DO at OBJ Bull, second to destroy enemy reserve and third to facilitate the passage of the 1st BDE, 25th ID. Critical to this mission is undetected movement to OBJ BULL and establishing a strong defense against the counterattack. The Battalion reconnaissance platoon and rifle squads conduct route and area reconnaissance to provide intelligence. The purpose of fire support is to destroy ADA systems, suppress enemy positions and provide obscuration to support the assault, and neutralize reinforcements. The purpose of movement and maneuver provided by engineers is to provide mobility for the battalion decisive operation onto OBJ BULL and then to provide mobility on RTE VIBE via reduction of obstacles ultimately allowing the passage of the 1st BDE, 25th ID. We are accepting risk in detaching a platoon from C Company, but we will mitigate this risk by providing them with priority of fires when it is time for them to seize their OBJ. At end-state, the companies will be in position to repel any counterattacks and the 1st BDE, 25th ID will pass unhindered along RTE VIBE and through our zone.
